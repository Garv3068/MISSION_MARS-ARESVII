Dr. Okafor pressed her face to the porthole on Day 431.
There it was — a mote of dust suspended in a sunbeam.

Blue. Achingly blue. The only blue thing in the entire visible universe from where she stood.

Oceans. Weather. Seven billion people who had no idea they were the most extraordinary thing in the cosmos.

She thought about the riverbed on Mars. The water that had flowed three billion years ago and gone silent. She thought about how close they had come to silence themselves.

And then she thought: we are still here. We are still going.
