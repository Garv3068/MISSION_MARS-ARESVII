WRITTEN EN ROUTE · DAYS 440–468 · ARES VII CREW
TO THE
NEXT ONES
We wrote this on the way home. Six of us, taking turns, passing the tablet around the common module on quiet nights when Earth was still small in the window.

We want you to know what it felt like.

It felt like standing at the edge of something enormous — not with fear, but with the deep, certain calm of people who belong exactly where they are. We stood on Mars. We breathed filtered air and wore borrowed atmosphere, but the ground under our boots was real, and the sky was real, and the silence was more real than anything any of us had ever heard.

The hardest part was not the isolation. It was not the dust storms, or the equipment failures, or the 22-minute delay that made every conversation with home feel like a letter rather than a call.

The hardest part was understanding what we were looking at. Three billion years of history pressed into every centimetre of rock. Rivers that ran before anything on Earth had learned to walk. We are not alone in time — only in the present moment.

That will change.

You who read this — you are either training for the next mission, or you are the children of someone who was. Maybe you are reading this in a habitat with a window that looks out onto a Martian plain, and the landscape outside is no longer strange to you. We hope it is no longer strange.

We hope you look at it the way we look at the sea. With love. With familiarity. With the knowledge that your ancestors crossed something impossible to bring you here.

Go further. Always go further.

— Commander Y. Chen, Eng. M. Kowalski, Dr. A. Okafor, Sgt. D. Reyes, Dr. S. Lund, Dr. P. Nair

HOME · 1 AU FROM THE SUN · THE ONLY BLUE WORLD
THE PALE
BLUE HOME
From 400,000 kilometres out, Earth looked like a marble someone had dropped and never picked up.

It had no idea it was the most improbable thing in the cosmos. It had no idea that six of its children had just spent 469 days learning the shape of another world so it could one day follow them there.

It was home. It was enough. And it was just the beginning.
